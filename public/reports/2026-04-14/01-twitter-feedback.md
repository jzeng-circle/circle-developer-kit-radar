## Executive Summary

Response to the App Kits launch was strongly positive. Developers consistently highlighted two themes: relief at having bridge, swap, and send unified in a single SDK, and excitement about built-in monetization. Several builders signaled active intent to integrate. One substantive technical question about fee atomicity went unanswered and warrants follow-up in documentation.

---

### 2. Built-in Monetization — Top Differentiator

Revenue sharing baked into transaction flows was called out more than any other single feature. Developers see it as a meaningful competitive edge.

> *"Built-in rev share is the cheat code"*
> — @prateekhh (6,448 followers)

> *"developers can configure revenue sharing directly in transaction flows without deploying custom contracts... this is a revolutionary approach"*
> — @tp058181 (317 followers)

> *"Clean infra usually wins flow over time. If App Kits nails UX + fees on $USDC routes, devs won't chase fragmented liquidity as much. Monetized rails baked in is a quiet edge"*
> — @CRBOTSignals (1,665 followers)

**Takeaway**: Monetization is a differentiator worth amplifying in marketing and docs. It addresses a real gap — most SDKs make revenue sharing an afterthought.

---

### 3. Open Technical Question — Fee Atomicity

One developer asked a specific, substantive question that received no reply:

> *"The built-in monetization feature looks great. Are the configured fees collected within the same atomic transaction, or does it require a separate contract interaction? I'm curious about the impact on gas overhead, especially for high-frequency payment workflows."*
> — @Albatros_0x (894 followers, 1 like)

**Takeaway**: This is a legitimate concern for production builders. It should be addressed in the FAQ or docs. If fees are atomic, that's a strong selling point to state explicitly.
